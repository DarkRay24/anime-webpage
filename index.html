    What is "Anime"?

    The word anime — pronounced "ah-knee-may" — is an abbreviation of the word animation. In Japan, the word is used to refer to all animation. However, outside of Japan, it has become the catch-all term for animation from Japan.
    Most anime fans can sum this up in two words: "It's different." Anime is as unlike most American cartoons like "Batman" and "Spider-Man" are different from the comics that run in daily papers. Confused about where to start? We recommend "Death Note" and "Naruto" for starters. If you already know a friend who's an anime fan, clue them in on what you like to watch — they should be able to guide you best.

  • Manga

    What are Mangas?

    Manga is an umbrella term for a wide variety of comic books and graphic novels originally produced and published in Japan. Unlike American comic books, which are usually printed in full color, Japanese manga are almost always published in black and white. Full-color prints are often only used for special releases.

    The main difference between them and American comics is that manga make up a large percentage of the Japanese publishing market, so that there are comics available for all ages, genders and interests. Golf manga, football manga, fishing, superheroes, aliens, true crime, action...whatever a person might like, there is probably a manga about it.

    What's so Great about them?

    Manga has a completely different distribution model than comics. Most manga is serialized in a publication - or, now, online - which allows for a longer, more complicated story to be told, with arcs for character development. Comics are sold as stand-alone items, with less and less page count per issue, to keep costs down, so less and less space for a story.

    Manga is created and told by the same team from beginning to end. Comics are jobbed out to different creative teams as the publisher wants. So every 6 or 12 issues, to shake up the sales, they switch writers and artists, which means the previous year’s worth of story is retconned or rebooted to make way for a new one.
